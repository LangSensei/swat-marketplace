# DWR System Comparison

## Reading Guide

- Use this table to compare chemistry families, not to identify exact commercial recipes.
- Water repellency, oil repellency, wash durability, hand feel, and compliance rarely optimize at the same time.

## Comparison Table

| Chemistry family | Water repellency | Oil repellency | Durability after laundering | Hand feel | Relative cost | Current regulatory position | Typical fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C8 fluorocarbon (legacy) | Excellent | Excellent | Excellent | Clean and versatile | High | Obsolete or heavily restricted; not suitable for new textile programs | Legacy benchmark only |
| C6 fluorocarbon | Very good to excellent | Moderate to good | Very good | Usually cleaner than wax systems | Medium to high | Transitional and under growing pressure from state PFAS bans and broader PFAS restrictions | Short-term legacy support where still legally allowed |
| Fluorine-free silicone | Good to very good | Weak | Moderate to good | Soft to slick | Medium | Generally preferred for PFAS-free programs | Fashion outerwear, soft handle, water-only target |
| Fluorine-free wax or paraffin | Moderate to good | Weak | Moderate | Can feel fuller or slightly waxy | Low to medium | PFAS-free and commercially common | Cost-sensitive water repellency with limited oil resistance |
| Hyperbranched or dendrimer fluorine-free | Good to very good | Weak to modest | Good | Often cleaner than basic wax systems | Medium to high | PFAS-free and positioned as premium C0 technology | Higher-performance PFAS-free upgrades |
| PU or silicone hybrid fluorine-free | Good to very good | Weak | Good | Tunable; may preserve hand better than simple wax systems | Medium | PFAS-free and practical for durable C0 programs | Sportswear or technical apparel needing balanced durability and hand |

## Typical Supplier Examples

| Chemistry family | Common commercial positioning examples |
|---|---|
| C6 fluorocarbon | Legacy fluorocarbon DWR lines from major textile-chemical suppliers |
| Silicone fluorine-free | Soft-handle water repellents from Archroma, Rudolf, Tanatex, CHT, and others |
| Wax or paraffin fluorine-free | Cost-oriented C0 lines used for simple spray-rating targets |
| Hyperbranched or dendrimer C0 | Premium fluorine-free systems marketed for higher durability |
| PU or silicone hybrid C0 | Balanced-performance PFAS-free programs for apparel and outdoor categories |

## Decision Rules

1. If the customer needs **oil repellency**, say clearly that fluorine-free systems usually underperform fluorinated chemistry.
2. If the market is **PFAS-sensitive**, start from fluorine-free options and treat C6 as a risk-managed legacy path, not a default recommendation.
3. If **hand feel** is critical, compare silicone-rich systems against wax-heavy systems explicitly.
4. If **wash durability** is the main concern, evaluate cure conditions, fabric construction, and crosslinking package alongside the DWR family itself.
5. If the brief says **PFAS-free**, recommend verification by supplier declaration plus suitable fluorine-screening or analyte testing before making the final claim.

## Commercial Warnings

- Spray rating is not the same as oil repellency, hydrostatic head, or long-term field durability.
- Many PFAS-free systems can hit strong initial spray results but lose edge under abrasion, contamination, or repeated washing.
- Cure profile, residual surfactant, and fabric surface cleanliness can matter as much as the named DWR family.
