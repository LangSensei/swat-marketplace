# Auxiliary Chemical Brands

## Use Notes

- This file is for orientation, not for treating a supplier name as proof of exact chemistry.
- Product lines change by region, distributor, and year. Always confirm with the current SDS, TDS, and supplier declaration.

## Fast Reading Rules

1. If a brand is best known for **fluorinated performance chemistry**, assume PFAS due diligence is required before recommending it.
2. If a brand is positioned around **fluorine-free outdoor finishing**, compare durability and hand feel carefully against buyer expectations.
3. Parent-company recognition is not enough; use the **exact product line or SDS** whenever the compliance verdict matters.
4. A strong supplier name does not override the need to separate **finished-goods compliance**, **MRSL conformance**, and **performance fit**.

## Practical Questions to Ask When a Brand Name Appears

- Is this a finish chemistry, an auxiliary, or only an upstream raw material?
- Is the claim current, or is it legacy market reputation from an older fluorinated era?
- Does the market destination require a finished-goods PFAS position, a supplier declaration, or both?
- Is the product line optimized for water repellency only, or is oil repellency still being implied?
