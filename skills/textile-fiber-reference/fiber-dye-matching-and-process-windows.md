# Fiber Dye Matching and Process Windows

## Use Notes

- These are practical operating windows for woven synthetics, not universal mill recipes.
- Shade depth, machine type, oligomer load, and stretch content can move the best window materially.

## Matching Table

| Fiber | Main dye classes | Typical pH | Peak temperature, C | Pressure mode | Common auxiliary needs | Main watchpoints |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| PA6 | Acid dyes, 1:2 metal-complex dyes, selected polyamide-reactive systems | 4.5-6.0 for general leveling; lower for deep shades | 95-100 | Atmospheric or mild pressure | Leveling agent, anti-crease, acetic acid or ammonium buffering | Fast strike, barre risk, acid overshoot, yellowing on stretch blends |
| PA66 | Acid dyes, 1:2 metal-complex dyes, selected polyamide-reactive systems | 4.5-6.0 | 98-105 | Atmospheric or mild pressure | Similar to PA6, often with stronger leveling control | Slower diffusion than PA6, tighter levelness control needed, more heat tolerance but still moisture-sensitive |
| PET | Disperse dyes | 4.5-5.5 | 125-135 | HTHP for exhaust dyeing; thermosol for continuous routes | Dispersing agent, oligomer management, carrier only in legacy low-temperature routes, reduction clearing for medium and deep shades | Oligomer, shade change from heat history, poor wash-off, sublimation, hydrolysis if alkaline |
| PBT | Low- to medium-energy disperse dyes | 4.5-5.5 | 110-125 | Often lower-pressure than PET; HTHP still common | Dispersing and leveling support, gentle aftercleaning | Easier overheat damage than PET, softer structure, migration if ramp is too aggressive |
| CDP | Cationic dyes, sometimes modified disperse support systems | 4.0-5.0 | 110-125 | HTHP or boil depending construction | Retarder or leveling package, strict contamination control | Cationic and anionic incompatibility, streaking if strike is too fast, lower thermal ceiling than PET |
| Sorona or PTT family | Disperse dyes | 4.5-5.5 | 115-125 | HTHP common | Leveling and dispersing package | Lower thermal demand than PET, fast diffusion can hurt levelness, excessive heat can flatten stretch recovery |
| Elastic bicomponent polyester family (T-400 or T-800 type) | Disperse dyes chosen for the specific component mix | 4.5-5.5 | 115-125 | HTHP common | Strong leveling support and slower ramps | Differential uptake between components, barre, uneven stretch recovery after overheat |
| Spandex-containing blends | Usually dye the carrier fiber and protect the elastane | Mildly acidic to near-neutral unless carrier demands otherwise | Keep the blend at the lowest effective window; often below 120 when possible | Depends on carrier fiber | Anti-yellowing support, low-attack auxiliaries, careful wash chemistry | Chlorine damage, heat-set distortion, hydrolysis, loss of elastic recovery |

## Quick Rules by Fiber Family

### Polyamide family

- Levelness is usually a strike-control problem before it is a peak-temperature problem.
- pH drift matters because acid uptake can accelerate quickly once the bath becomes too sharp.
- For PA plus spandex blends, protect the elastane first and accept a narrower process window.

### Polyester family

- PET likes heat, but heat history also drives oligomer release, migration, and sublimation risk.
- PBT, PTT, and elastic bicomponent polyesters often need a gentler ramp than standard PET.
- If wash-off or rubbing fastness is weak on medium or deep disperse shades, reduction clearing should be considered explicitly.

### CDP

- Treat cationic systems as contamination-sensitive. Small amounts of incompatible chemistry can create streaking or shade drift.
- Thermal severity should stay lower than a standard PET mindset would suggest.

## Practical Curve Language

When reporting a process curve, express it in this order:

1. Pretreatment state and assumptions
2. Starting pH and liquor condition
3. Heat-up rate
4. Dosing points
5. Peak hold window
6. Cool-down logic
7. Aftertreatment and fastness-control step

That format is easier for buyers and mill counterparts to compare than a loose narrative paragraph.
